About Me

Hello! I'm Iris!

Biostatistics Ph.D. Candidate

I am a third-year Ph.D. candidate in biostatistics at New York University, where I am advised by Prof. Yang Feng. Prior to joining NYU, I obtained an M.S. in data science from University of Pennsylvania under the supervision of Prof. Kai Tan, and a B.S. in applied mathematics from Case Western Reserve University under the mentorship of Prof. Mark Meckes and Prof. Daniela Calvetti.

My research focuses on statistical machine learning and high-dimensional inference, with an emphasis on transfer learning, representation learning, and AI-driven methods for healthcare. I am interested in building theoretically grounded methodologies that enable robust and efficient learning from complex, heterogeneous, and large-scale biomedical data.
